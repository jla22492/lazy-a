# CREATIVE SPRINT 02 — Reveal the Person

Authorized by Jonathan after the Sprint 01 review; direction prepared with the
Creative Director. Ten implementation work orders (0046–0055), then one
comprehensive Creative Sprint Review and a full stop. This document extends —
never overrides — the constitution (docs/ORIENTATION.md).

---

## New Creative Locks

- 🔒 The room reveals personality through habits, not possessions.
- 🔒 A functioning room is more believable than a decorated room.
- 🔒 The browser interrupts a larger world — it does not contain one.
- 🔒 The room is rendered in real time. The impossible is rendered offline.
- 🔒 Surprise is the reward for believing the room is real.

## Guiding Principle

**Everything visible should imply something invisible.**

A cable implies a computer. A chair implies a person. A shadow implies an
object. A coffee ring implies yesterday. The browser is only showing us one
small piece of a much larger world.

---

## Mission

Sprint 01 answered: *"This is a creative workspace."*

Sprint 02 must begin answering: *"Whose workspace is this?"*

Not through branding. Through habits. Through infrastructure. Through
history. Through personality. This is no longer about adding objects — it is
about revealing the invisible life that exists beyond the frame.

Do not decorate. Reveal.

Ask constantly:

- What interrupted this person?
- Where do they always leave this?
- What happened here years ago?
- What exists outside this browser that I'm only seeing evidence of?

---

## Creative Direction (from Jonathan's Sprint 01 review)

- The room feels mid-work, but not yet personal.
- The right side feels too heavy and visually busy.
- The corners make the room feel staged instead of part of a larger environment.
- The room needs greater dimensionality from off-screen light and shadow.
- Warmth should come from evidence of people, not warmer lighting.
- The chair should imply someone just stood up, not invite the visitor to sit down.
- The tripod is too on-the-nose and should not remain the storytelling device it currently is.
- The plant still feels designed instead of inevitable.
- Infrastructure is missing: outlets, chargers, adapters, wiring, lamps, cable
  routing, power. Someone should be able to actually work here.
- Continue thinking in layers of time instead of layers of objects.

---

## Technical Direction

- Continue building the room entirely in React Three Fiber.
- Do not implement impossible moments yet. Do not implement the hero image
  animation. Continue preparing the stage.
- The rendering architecture is formal: **the room is rendered in real time;
  the impossible is rendered offline** (Runway / Blender / After Effects and
  similar), eventually entering the room as video textures and other
  authored surfaces. This sprint only continues building a room worthy of
  them.
