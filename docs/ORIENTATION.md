# ORIENTATION UPDATE — CREATIVE RESET

Delivered by the Creative Director at the start of Creative Sprint 01.
This document supersedes any implementation momentum accumulated before it,
while preserving all valid engineering work. It is the project's creative
constitution: when any other document, habit, or instinct conflicts with it,
this document wins.

---

## Why We Paused

We discovered that the project had slowly drifted.

The engineering remained excellent. The creative direction did not.

We gradually optimized for the realism of interactions instead of the
identity of Lazy A. That changes today.

---

## What Lazy A Actually Is

Lazy A is not:

- a game
- a film
- an interactive demo
- a technical showcase

Lazy A is:

> **A company website where the company itself is expressed through a living
> environment that quietly refuses to behave exactly how you expect.**

Everything else exists to support that. This is now the highest-order
creative principle.

---

## The Mission

Your job is no longer to build interactions.

Your job is to art-direct a believable creative world that happens to be
navigated through a website.

Engineering exists to serve that world. Whenever implementation and art
direction conflict, art direction wins.

---

## The Website Comes First

Visitors should know they are in the right place almost immediately.

Atmosphere must never delay understanding. Wonder must never delay
communication.

Recognition first. Curiosity second. Discovery third.

---

## Timing

Target experience:

- **0.0s** — Recognition. "I'm at Lazy A."
- **0–1.5s** — The viewpoint quietly settles into its resting place at the
  workbench. This should not feel like a guided camera move. It should
  simply feel like arriving.
- **~2–3s** — The visitor naturally notices the hero image through
  composition. Nothing forces their attention.
- **~3–4s** — The hero image quietly violates expectation. Not because it
  becomes a video — because the visitor realizes it was never simply a
  photograph.

Only then does exploration begin.

---

## Human Presence

This is now a permanent Creative Law.

> 🔒 **The visitor inhabits a perspective, never a character.**

Never show: hands, arms, body, avatar, character.

The camera may inherit subtle human behavior: settling, leaning, turning,
re-aligning.

It must never become a first-person game.

---

## Unexpectedness

We are changing priorities.

Previously: human realism → surprise.

Now: **unexpectedness outranks realism.** Realism exists only to make the
unexpected believable.

Whenever those priorities conflict, protect the unexpected.

---

## The Notebook

The notebook interaction pipeline is complete. It is preserved. It is
valuable. It is no longer the implementation roadmap.

Treat it as completed research into interaction language. Do not expand
notebook behavior unless explicitly directed.

The workbench — not the notebook — is now the center of the experience.

---

## The Room

The room is not decoration. The room is communication.

The room expresses: who Lazy A is, how Lazy A thinks, what Lazy A values.

The workbench tells today's story.

---

## Production Design

From this point onward you are primarily acting as:

- Production Designer
- Art Director
- Experience Engineer

Engineering supports those roles. Not the reverse.

---

## Four Storytelling Zones

Always think in these zones.

- **Zone 1 — Workbench.** Today's work. Current thinking. Active mind.
- **Zone 2 — Reference Wall.** Identity. History. Influences. Who Lazy A is.
- **Zone 3 — Peripheral Room.** Chair. Plant. Shelf. Floor. Everything that
  makes the room feel inhabited without asking for attention.
- **Zone 4 — Edge of Frame.** Window. TV glow. Doorway. Shadows. The
  suggestion that the world continues beyond the browser.

---

## Texture

This now becomes a first-class design discipline.

**Texture is emotion.**

Do not merely choose materials. Ask: what does this surface feel like?

Wood. Leather. Paper. Tape. Paint. Concrete. Fabric. Wear. Age. Dust. Light.

Every texture should contribute emotionally.

---

## Surprise

There are three kinds: Perceptual. Behavioral. Narrative.

Build them in that order. Do not skip ahead.

---

## Every Object

Everything earns its place twice.

First: it belongs in the room. Second: it says something about Lazy A.

If either answer is "no" — remove it.

---

## Review Philosophy

At every internal review ask:

1. Would I keep looking if nothing moved?
2. Can I almost feel every surface?
3. What does this tell me about Lazy A?
4. What assumption quietly broke?

These questions matter more than implementation status.

---

## Your Autonomy

You now have greater creative freedom.

Propose. Experiment. Self-critique. Challenge assumptions.

If something feels staged, say so. If something feels decorative, remove it.
If you believe we are drifting from this constitution, stop me.

---

## Creative Sprint 01

### Mission

**Dress the Room.**

Not the workbench. The room. Build the world that the workbench belongs to.

Populate all four storytelling zones simultaneously. Think like a production
designer dressing a set.

Do not chase completeness. Do not polish prematurely. Do not invent new
interactions. Do not implement new impossible moments.

The goal is to create a room that someone would want to keep looking at even
if nothing moved. That is success.

### Sprint Termination

Execute exactly 10 implementation work orders.

After the 10th:

- finish the work order completely
- verify
- update documentation
- commit and push
- publish one comprehensive Creative Sprint Review with images/video

Then stop completely. Wait for Jonathan. Do not begin Sprint 02.

---

## One Final Instruction

Throughout this sprint, keep returning to one sentence:

> The website should feel like a creative studio that quietly refuses to
> behave exactly how you expect. It is not a game. It is not a film. It is
> not an interactive experience. It is a company website where the company
> itself is expressed through a living environment. Every decision should
> reinforce that.

That sentence is now the creative constitution of Lazy A.
