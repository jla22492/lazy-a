# CREATIVE SPRINT 03 — The Room Becomes a Place

Authorized by Jonathan after the Sprint 02 review; direction prepared with the
Creative Director. Fifteen implementation work orders (0056–0070), internal
reviews after 0060, 0065, and 0070, then one comprehensive Creative Sprint
Review and a full stop. This document extends the constitution
(docs/ORIENTATION.md) and Sprint 02's brief (docs/SPRINT_02.md).

---

## Mission

The room is no longer being dressed. Now it becomes unmistakably someone's
place. Not a creative studio — **Lazy A's** creative studio. Not by adding
"filmmaker objects," but by revealing the life that produces the work.

Jonathan's feedback is creative direction, not a checklist: build toward the
feeling, not the objects.

---

## New Creative Locks

- 🔒 The room is discovered, never decorated.
- 🔒 Warmth comes from accumulated life, not visual styling.
- 🔒 The visitor should wonder what exists beyond the frame more often than
  what exists inside it.

(Reaffirmed from prior sprints: everything visible implies something
invisible; the camera inherits human behavior and never becomes a human
character; the room is rendered in real time and the impossible offline;
evidence of light, never lighting.)

---

## Jonathan's emotional corrections

Not object requests — corrections to the feeling:

- The room still feels generic rather than personal — anyone's workspace,
  not Lazy A's.
- The right side still carries too much visual weight.
- The room still feels too self-contained.
- Warmth should come from history, not decoration.
- The chair should imply someone just stood up, not invite the visitor to sit.
- The world outside the frame should become increasingly believable.
- The upward direction — higher on the walls, closer to the ceiling — is
  largely unexplored.

Think constantly: What was this person doing five minutes ago? What did they
do here last year? What room continues outside this frame?

---

## Process

- Autonomy raised: internal Creative Director reviews only after 0060, 0065,
  and 0070. No intermediate check-ins unless genuinely drifting.
- At each review, answer honestly: What surprised me? What feels inevitable?
  What still feels designed instead of discovered? If I removed three things
  immediately, what would I remove?
- We are not building toward the impossible yet. We are building a room that
  deserves the impossible: if every magical moment were deleted, the room
  should still be worth looking at.

## Termination

Execute exactly fifteen work orders (0056–0070), committing and pushing each.
At 0070: stop completely; produce one comprehensive Creative Sprint Review
(before/after stills, a short resting film, and an honest artistic assessment
of where the room still falls short). No Work Order 0071 under any
circumstances until Jonathan reviews Sprint 03.
